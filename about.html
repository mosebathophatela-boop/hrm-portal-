How It Started

FROM A FRUSTRATING MONDAY

It was a rainy Monday in October 2022. Maya Chen had just sat through her fourth back-to-back Zoom call of the day, typed the same status update into three different tools, and realised she hadn't done a single minute of actual work.

She called her old colleague Jordan Park, a software engineer who'd been building AI tools in his spare time. "What if we just fixed this?" she said. Three months later, Spark Labs was born in Maya's apartment with a laptop, a whiteboard, and a very strong opinion about the future of work.

"We didn't set out to build a company. We set out to solve a problem so obvious it was embarrassing no one had fixed it."

— Maya Chen, Co-Founder & CEO
